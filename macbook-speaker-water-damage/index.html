Frequently Asked Questions

These questions address the specific recovery situation this page covers.

What should I do first if liquid gets near a MacBook speaker?

Power off the MacBook immediately using the power button, not from the menu. Do not press other keys or open apps. Tilt the MacBook so the affected side faces down to encourage liquid to drain away from the logic board rather than toward it. Wipe any surface liquid gently with a dry cloth and leave the lid open at 90 degrees. Do not put it in a bag or close it while wet.

Can a browser-based speaker eject tool work on a MacBook?

Yes. You can run Fix My Speaker in Safari or Chrome on the MacBook, which will play the eject tone through the built-in speakers if the audio system is still functional after the exposure. This is worth trying for mild splashes. For significant liquid exposure, the priority is protecting the logic board first before testing audio output.

When should I take a MacBook to Apple or a repair shop after liquid exposure?

Any time liquid reaches the keyboard, ports, or fan vents rather than just the speaker grille. MacBook logic board damage from liquid is not always immediate, and corrosion can develop over days. If the MacBook shows any unexpected behaviour after liquid exposure, such as key failures, screen flickering, or charging issues, professional inspection should happen before the problem worsens.
